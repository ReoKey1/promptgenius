1. The Pain of Vague Prompts: Generic Outputs and Wasted Time

The most frequent mistake in prompt engineering is a lack of specificity. A vague prompt like, "Write an article about the benefits of our new software," is an open invitation for the AI to produce a bland, generic list of features without any compelling narrative or audience focus. This happens because the AI lacks the necessary context to understand your true intent. It doesn't know your target audience, the desired tone, the key pain points your software solves, or the unique value proposition you want to highlight.

This ambiguity forces you into a reactive loop of endless revisions, where you spend more time correcting the AI's output than you would have spent writing the content yourself. The core issue is the absence of a feedback mechanism to guide you before you generate the content.
